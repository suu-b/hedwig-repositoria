# Exercise 1 — Defining a System

## Prompt

*Identify a system from your daily life. Define its boundary, list its key elements, and describe at least two feedback loops operating within it.*

---

## Essay

The morning routine is a system most people inhabit without ever examining. Its boundary is roughly defined by waking up and leaving the house — or, more honestly, by the moment the phone is first touched.

**Elements:** alarm, sleep inertia, caffeine dependency, to-do anxiety, social media pull, hunger signals, time pressure.

**Feedback Loop 1 — Reinforcing (vicious):** Poor sleep → slow cognition → longer time on trivial decisions → later start → anxiety → poor sleep. This is the loop that grinds people down over years without them realising it is a *loop* and not merely a series of bad days.

**Feedback Loop 2 — Balancing:** Time pressure → reduced dithering → faster task completion → reduced pressure. The deadline creates its own cure, but only up to a threshold. Past that threshold, it collapses into paralysis.

**Observation:** What strikes me is that most interventions people attempt — productivity apps, morning routines, habit trackers — target individual elements without touching the *structure* of the loops. They are pulling at leaves while the roots remain.
